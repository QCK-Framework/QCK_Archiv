* **Summary:** This paper builds upon the theory of "Topological Redshift" and provides empirical validation using current astronomical data (JWST, SH0ES). It strictly distinguishes between local motion (Doppler) and global light interaction (impedance). The central thesis is that cosmological redshift is not a consequence of expansion (stretching of space), but a frequency filtering by the vacuum lattice. The paper shows that observations regarding surface brightness (Tolman test) and the angular diameter of distant galaxies are more compatible with a static, Euclidean geometry than with the standard model of expansion.
* **Axioms:**
  1. **Scale Separation:** Local redshift is kinetic (Doppler), global redshift is medium-based (impedance/dissipation).
  2. **Static Geometry:** The universe does not expand. It exists in a static steady state. Redshift is energy loss, not stretching.
* **Formulas:**
  * **Geometric Coupling Factor:** The local Hubble constant is the CMB value, amplified by the lattice structure near matter.
    `H_local = H_CMB * (1 + 1/12)`
  * **Numerical Validation:**
    `73.04 approx 67.4 * 1.0833`
* **Results:**
  * **Tolman Test (Surface Brightness):** JWST data show galaxies at high `z` whose brightness does not decrease with `(1+z)^4` as expected, but remains consistently high. This supports the static model.
  * **Little Red Dots (Angular Diameter):** The discovery of extremely compact galaxies at high redshift contradicts the "ballooning effect" (turnaround) of the expansion model and confirms the Euclidean perspective (`size propto 1/distance`).
  * **Time Dilation as Dispersion:** The apparent slow-motion of supernovae is explained as signal broadening (dispersion) through the lattice, analogous to signals in fiber optic cables, not as time dilation.
* **Philosophical Context:** The paper is the empirical "showdown" of the QCK framework with the standard model. It argues that expansion is an unnecessary additional assumption ("epicycle") that can be replaced by the simpler explanation of vacuum friction. The precision of the Hubble prediction is considered the "smoking gun" for the lattice structure.
