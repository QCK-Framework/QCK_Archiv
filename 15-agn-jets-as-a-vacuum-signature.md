* **Summary:** This paper provides a quantum physical solution to two main problems of Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN): The "Launching Problem" (How are jets initially accelerated?) and the "Composition Problem" (What are they made of?). Because the local vacuum is "elevated" by surrounding matter, the difference in energy is directly converted into kinetic energy. * **Axioms:**

  3. **The Genesis Program:** The emergence of matter (neutrons) from the Chaos Dimension always occurs with a fixed value of `E_Genesis = +940 MeV`.

    `E_vak(rho) = -938 MeV + Delta_E(rho)`
    (In our vicinity, `Delta_E approx +438 MeV`, leading to `-500 MeV`).
  * **The Energetic Mismatch (Kinetic Energy):**
    `KE = E_Genesis - |E_vak(rho)|`
  * **Minimum Launch Velocity (local):**
    `KE_min = 940 - 500 = 440 MeV`
    `v_min approx 0.7314 c` (This is the theoretical lower limit for any jet).
  * **Logarithmic Density Coupling:**
    `Delta_E(rho) = Delta_E0 * ln(1 + alpha * rho/rho_0)` (where `alpha approx 1.06`).
* **Results:**

Since neutrons travel approx. 1.9 AU at `0.73c` before decaying (`n -> p + e + nu`), the charged, magnetically gripable component only forms at this distance. This explains why jets collimate only at a certain distance from the black hole.
  * **Velocity Correlation:** From the measured velocities of Seyfert galaxies (`0.8c`) and Quasars (`>0.9c`), the model precisely reconstructs the matter density in the core. Higher density = Higher vacuum plateau = More excess energy = Faster jet.
  * **Internal Growth Channel:** A portion of neutron emergence occurs *inside* the event horizon. This allows black holes an "in-situ growth" beyond the Eddington limit, explaining the existence of supermassive black holes in the early universe.
* **Philosophical Context:**
  The paper links high-energy astrophysics with vacuum ontology. A jet is not a "cannon" firing matter, but a "leak" in the vacuum through which matter breaks in with excess energy. The velocity of the jet is the direct voltmeter for the local vacuum tension.

* **Zusammenfassung:**
